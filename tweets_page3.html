Q. When trading forex, bitcoin, stocks or commodity futures what's considered a good Profit Factor, Sortino Ratio & Sharpe Ratio? P.S. Webtrading domain is available >> https://bit.ly/3bN1VXO << Profit Factor is defined as amount of profit per dollar vs drawdown. A profit factor of 1.5 to 2.0 or more is considered good.

Sortino Ratio measures risk-adjusted return of investment, only considering downside volatility. A Sortino ratio ranging from 1.0 to 2.0 or more is considered good. Some trading experts now believe Sortino to be superior vs the better known Sharpe.

Sharpe Ratio measures excess return (above risk-free rate) per unit of total risk. A Sharpe ratio from 0.75 to 1.0 or better is considered good. P.S. These 3 valuable trading tools are free in TradingView when doing strategy deep back-testing and performace summary.
